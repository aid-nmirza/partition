After partition, English and Hindi became the national languages of India. The prioritisation of English in official forums gave India a diplomatic advantage in normalising relationships with the British Empire, while also accomodating the foundling nation's immense ethnic diversity. Although English was intended to be phased out from use in Congress after 15 years, advocates from non-Hindi speaking regions insisted on maintaining the use of English in nation-wide legislature.

Hindi, like Urdu, is a name for the language described as Hindustani by linguists. While formal registers, poetic language, and written script of Urdu and Hindi diverge, the every-day forms of these languages are mutually intelligible and largely indistinguisable from one another.
