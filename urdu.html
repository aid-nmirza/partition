After partition, Urdu and English became the national languages of Pakistan. The prioritisation of Urdu in official forums gave Pakistan a diplomatic advantage in establishing its independence as a nation state, while also demonstrating the foundling nation's immense cultural wealth. Although Urdu was intended to be the primary official language, English was the first language of many of the nation's founding cabinet.

Urdu, like Hindi, is a name for the language described as Hindustani by linguists. While formal registers, poetic language, and written script of Urdu and Hindi diverge, the every-day forms of these languages are mutually intelligible and largely indistinguisable from one another.
